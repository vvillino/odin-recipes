Panuozzo

Description

A form of street-food sandwich from Campania, served hot

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Bread Flour
  • 680 gr Cold refrigerator water
  • 3 gr Fresh brewers yeast or 1 dry
  • 25 gr Salt
  • Cheeses of your choice (mozzarella, provola)
  • fresh tomatoes
  • mixed salad
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. A spiral machine was used for the dough but it can be done both by hand and with a planetary mixer.
  2. Then add 90% of the water and await absorption by slightly increasing the speed (105 rpm). Once absorbed, add the salt and slowly add the remaining missing water, increasing the speed to 125 rpm.
  3. In 18 minutes dough closed, 25°
  4. I form a single loaf and put it in a closed oiled container for 1 hour at room temperature.
  5. After that I put it in the fridge for 16 hours
  6. Removed from the fridge, cut after 30 minutes and placed in starch for 6 hours and 30 minutes,
  7. Took 3 panielli weighing about 220 grams (with the others I made Neapolitans) spread without creating a ledge, giving an elongated shape
  8. Baked in Effeuno p134h at 400°, when it swells well I placed the paddle on the upper part of the Neapolitan panuozzo to prevent it from burning by going too close to the upper heating element
  9. Heated up today at 200° for 5/7 minutes turning it from time to time
